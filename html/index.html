Design Notes & Suggestions

Dashboard — What to show

Based on your schema, the home dashboard should show:

  • Student name, class, school, photo
  • Card status (Active / Inactive / Pending)
  • Card toggle → triggers OTP before deactivating
  • Blood group chip (always visible, critical for emergencies)
  • Last 2–3 scan events (from ScanLog)
  • Quick action buttons: QR, Edit, Emergency Contacts, History

New User Registration Flow

  • Step 1 — Scan QR or enter card number
  • Step 2 — Enter mobile → OTP → account created
  • Step 3 — Banner prompts to complete profile (non-blocking)
  • Step 4 — Edit form (all fields from EmergencyProfile)
  • Step 5 — OTP re-verify before saving sensitive data
  • Step 6 — Profile view → Card toggle → OTP → Card activated

Bottom Tab Bar

  • HOME – Dashboard with student card + activity
  • QR – Display the student's QR card full screen
  • SCAN – Floating center button, open camera scanner
  • UPDATE – Edit profile / pending update requests (StudentUpdateRequest)
  • SETTINGS – Notifications, visibility, account

Photo Placeholder (your suggestion)

The student card has a photo placeholder that shows initials until a photo is uploaded. Once uploaded, it displays the student photo. This ties to photo_url in the Student model. The avatar doubles as identity confirmation when a first responder scans the card.

Premium Button

The top-right Premium button opens an upgrade modal. For premium users it shows "PRO" with a gold star badge. This maps to the Subscription model — check subscription status on app load and badge accordingly.

Card Activate / Deactivate

Toggling the card active/inactive is a sensitive action. Always trigger an OTP challenge before confirming the change. Map the token status: ACTIVE ↔ INACTIVE. Show a bottom sheet confirmation with a timer before the OTP screen.
